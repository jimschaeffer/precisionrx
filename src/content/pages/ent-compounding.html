ENT Compounding for Nasal and Sinus Conditions in Birmingham

Ear, nose, and throat conditions often require localized, targeted treatment that commercially available medications can't always deliver effectively. At Precision Rx Compounding in Vestavia Hills, we prepare custom compounded ENT medications for patients across the Birmingham area, working with otolaryngologists, allergists, and primary care physicians to formulate preparations that address nasal, sinus, and ear conditions with precision.

ENT Compounds We Prepare

Nasal Irrigations and Rinses

Custom nasal irrigation solutions are one of the most commonly requested ENT compounds at Precision Rx. While saline rinses are available commercially, many patients with chronic sinusitis, post-surgical nasal care needs, or persistent ENT conditions benefit from irrigations that incorporate one or more active ingredients — such as antibiotics, antifungals, anti-inflammatories, or mucolytics — directly into the rinse solution.

Compounded nasal irrigations allow an otolaryngologist to prescribe a tailored solution that delivers medication where it's needed most — the nasal passages and sinus cavities — with minimal systemic exposure. This approach is particularly effective for patients managing chronic rhinosinusitis, post-operative sinus care, or recurrent nasal infections that have not responded adequately to oral medications.

Nasal Sprays

Compounded nasal sprays can combine multiple active ingredients — such as antihistamines, decongestants, corticosteroids, or mucosal protectants — in a single preparation, simplifying treatment regimens for patients managing allergic rhinitis, nasal polyps, or chronic congestion. Commercially available nasal sprays are limited in their ingredient combinations and concentrations; compounding removes those limitations.

Ear Drops

Custom compounded ear drops are commonly prescribed for patients with outer ear infections (otitis externa), chronic ear conditions, or post-surgical ear care. Compounded otic preparations can include antibiotics, antifungals, analgesics, and anti-inflammatory agents in the specific concentration and volume prescribed by a physician, and can be formulated without alcohol or other ingredients that cause irritation in sensitive patients.

Who Benefits From Compounded ENT Medications?

  • Patients with chronic sinusitis who need ongoing nasal irrigation with active medication
  • Post-surgical ENT patients requiring targeted nasal or ear wound care preparations
  • Patients with recurrent ear infections that have not responded to standard treatment
  • Allergy patients who need a multi-ingredient nasal spray not available commercially
  • Patients sensitive to the inactive ingredients or preservatives in commercial ENT products
