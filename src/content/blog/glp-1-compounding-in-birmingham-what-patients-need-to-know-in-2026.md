## What Are GLP-1 Medications?

GLP-1 stands for glucagon-like peptide-1 — a hormone your body naturally produces to regulate blood sugar and appetite. GLP-1 receptor agonists are a class of medications that mimic this hormone, and they've become widely known for their effectiveness in both type 2 diabetes management and weight loss.

You've likely heard the brand names: **Ozempic**, **Wegovy**, and **Mounjaro** are among the most recognized. The active ingredients behind these drugs — semaglutide and tirzepatide — are what compounding pharmacies work with when commercial products are unavailable or inaccessible.

## What the FDA Says About GLP-1 Compounding in 2026

The regulatory landscape around GLP-1 compounding has shifted in 2026. Here's the current picture:

The FDA has been updating its shortage list for semaglutide and tirzepatide, and compounding is legally permitted while these drugs remain on the shortage list. However, the FDA has also issued guidance warning patients about compounded GLP-1 products from facilities that are not properly licensed or that use salt forms of the active ingredient rather than the base compound.

**What this means for you:** Always ask your compounding pharmacy whether they use the base form of semaglutide or tirzepatide, and whether they operate as a 503A pharmacy (patient-specific compounding) or a 503B outsourcing facility. ## Who Is a Good Candidate for GLP-1 Therapy?

GLP-1 medications are typically prescribed for:

- Adults with type 2 diabetes who need better blood sugar control
- Adults with a BMI of 30 or higher (or 27+ with a weight-related condition) seeking medically supervised weight loss
- Patients who have tried lifestyle changes without adequate results
- Patients whose physicians recommend GLP-1 therapy as part of a broader metabolic health plan

GLP-1 medications are **not appropriate for everyone**. Patients with a personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or multiple endocrine neoplasia syndrome type 2 should not use these medications. Your prescribing physician is the right person to determine candidacy.
