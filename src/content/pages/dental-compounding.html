Dental Compounding

Dental procedures and oral health conditions often require medications that commercial products can't adequately address — whether it's a topical anesthetic in a specific concentration, an antimicrobial preparation for an oral infection, or a custom mouth rinse for a patient with sensitivities. At Precision Rx Compounding in Vestavia Hills, we prepare custom dental compounds for patients and dental practices across Birmingham, working directly with dentists, oral surgeons, and periodontists to support outstanding patient care.

Dental Compounds We Prepare

Topical Anesthetics and Numbing Creams

Topical anesthetics are among the most commonly compounded dental preparations. Commercial topical anesthetics are available in limited concentrations and flavors, which doesn't suit every patient or procedure. Compounded topical numbing creams can be formulated at the exact concentration a dentist requires, in a flavored or unflavored base, and can combine multiple anesthetic agents — such as lidocaine, benzocaine, and tetracaine — for deeper or longer-lasting topical anesthesia.

These preparations are particularly valuable for pediatric dental patients, patients with needle phobia, and procedures requiring extended topical anesthesia before injection. Custom flavoring options — including bubblegum, mint, cherry, and others — improve patient comfort and acceptance, particularly for younger patients.

Oral Infection Treatments

Oral infections — including periodontal infections, pericoronitis, oral ulcers, and post-extraction infections — sometimes require targeted antimicrobial or antifungal treatment that goes beyond what commercial products offer. Compounded preparations for oral infections can include antibiotics, antifungals, anti-inflammatories, and analgesics in mouth rinses, gels, or ointments applied directly to the affected tissue.

For patients with oral candidiasis, recurrent aphthous ulcers, or periodontal disease requiring sustained antimicrobial exposure, compounded preparations allow a dentist or periodontist to prescribe exactly what the clinical situation calls for — not the closest commercially available option.

Dry Socket Preparations

Patients who develop alveolar osteitis — commonly known as dry socket — following tooth extraction require analgesic and protective dressing to support healing and pain relief. Compounded dry socket preparations can be formulated with the specific analgesic, anti-inflammatory, and antimicrobial agents a dentist prescribes, in a delivery form designed for placement in the extraction site.

Mouth Rinses and Oral Gels

Custom compounded mouth rinses and oral gels can be prepared for a wide range of conditions including mucositis, oral lichen planus, denture sore spots, and post-radiation oral care. For patients undergoing cancer treatment, compounded oral preparations — sometimes called "magic mouthwash" formulations — combine anesthetic, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory agents in a single rinse to manage treatment-related oral side effects.
